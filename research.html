Contact Surfaces Visualization - Video

In the video we visualize the contact surfaces in the configuration space of a triangular robot which translates and rotates amid planar polygons. You can here see the various possible contacts and their three-dimensional representation. The visualization is based on an explicit parameterization of the contact surfaces that we developed.

The video was accepted to SoCG 2012 and will be presented at Chapel Hill on June 20th 2012. A repository of videos and multimedia project which were presented in SoCG along the years is available in the Computational Geometry Pages, and the accpeted projects of 2012 can be found here.

Contact Surfaces Analysis and Motion Planning

Given a polygonal robot moving amid polygonal obstacles in the plane, I consider the configuration space and the contact surface embedded in this space. A point on such a contact surface represents a configuration where either a vertex of the robot touches an edge of an obstacle, or an edge of the robot touches a vertex of an obstacle. Understanding and exploring the configuration space is of tremendous importance in motion planning.

2D and 3D Point Sets

In many applications one has to deal with point sets, for example when processing the output of a 3D scan. At the moment I am investigating the following issues:
  1. Approximate the intrinsic/geodesic metric.
  2. Approximate the Cut-Locus of a 2D or 3D shape. In the JavaView applet to the right one can see this approximation. Rotate and zoom in order to find the green vertex for which the cut locus is approximated. Here you can find another example.
  3. Approximate the topological properties of the underlying domain.
